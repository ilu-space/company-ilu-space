01 · Problem

Nordic Care Facilities Face a Loneliness Crisis — at Scale

Finnish eldercare facilities are under structural pressure: rising resident numbers, stretched staff-to-resident ratios, and a growing population of elderly people who spend large parts of their day without meaningful social interaction. Loneliness among care home residents is a documented clinical problem — it accelerates cognitive decline, increases depression, and creates additional burden on care staff who are already at capacity.

Existing digital companionship tools were either screen-based (requiring tech literacy and dexterity that many residents don't have) or passive (background music, TV). There was a gap for something that could have a genuine conversation, remember the resident, and report back to carers — in a form that didn't require the resident to learn anything new.

02 · The Approach

A Physical Companion That Meets Residents Where They Are

The core design decision was physical presence. A voice-only device (like a smart speaker) lacks the social cues that trigger engagement. A screen-based device requires active operation. Qluve was designed as a compact robot with a physical form — friendly, non-threatening, present in the room — that initiates conversation and responds naturally to voice.

  • Interaction is entirely voice-driven: no screen to navigate, no buttons to press. Residents simply talk to it.
  • The AI is personalized per resident — it learns preferences, remembers past conversations, and can discuss topics the resident actually cares about (family, history, hobbies).
  • Language is not a barrier: with over 50 languages supported, residents whose first language isn't Finnish (a common reality in care homes serving immigrant populations) can interact in their native tongue.
  • Care staff get a dashboard showing activity data — when residents engaged, for how long, and any notable signals — so caregivers can act on information, not guesses.
03 · The Stack

Hardware to Dashboard — End to End

This is the build that demonstrates what "full-stack AI robotics" actually means in practice. Most companies can do the software layer. Ilu Space built every layer.

Physical Hardware

Custom robot design, 3D-printed enclosures, embedded microcontroller integration. Designed for care home environments: durable, hygienic surfaces, compact footprint.

Embedded Systems

On-device firmware handling microphone input, speaker output, local processing, and network communication. Low-latency response cycle to make conversation feel natural.

AI Voice Layer

Large language model integration with voice synthesis and speech recognition. Multilingual support across 50+ languages. Persona and memory layers for resident personalization.

Cloud Infrastructure

Device management, conversation logging, per-resident data storage, real-time sync with caregiver dashboard. Secure, GDPR-compliant data handling.

Caregiver Dashboard

Web application giving care staff visibility into resident engagement. Activity logs, engagement patterns, configurable alerts. Designed for non-technical users.

Deployment Pipeline

Remote firmware update capability, fleet management for multiple devices across multiple facilities. Zero-downtime updates to AI models and conversation logic.

05 · What We Learned

What Actually Matters When AI Meets Care

Physical presence changes the interaction completely

Residents who wouldn't engage with a voice assistant on a tablet engaged readily with a robot in the room. The physical form isn't decoration — it's the thing that makes the interaction feel social rather than transactional.

Language support is equity, not a feature

A meaningful portion of care home residents in Finland have non-Finnish first languages. Building 50+ language support wasn't scope creep — it was the difference between a product that works for everyone and one that works for most people.

Staff adoption matters as much as resident adoption

The caregiver dashboard wasn't an afterthought — it was what enabled care staff to trust the system. When carers can see what happened and act on the data, they become advocates rather than skeptics. AI that generates data no one sees is AI that gets switched off.

The deployment complexity is where most AI products fail

Getting AI to work on a device in a care facility — with reliable networking, over-the-air updates, regulatory-compliant data handling, and staff who aren't developers — is harder than the AI itself. The full-stack build was the product.
